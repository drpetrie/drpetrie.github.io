* Gladys Cooper was born in North Dakota in 1915 to parents of Pennsylvania and Bohemian (Czech) ancestry. She grew up during the tail end of the pioneer farming era and the height of the Great Depression. Her early life was marked by economic hardship, movement, and resilience.
* She met her future husband, Robert Petrie, at a dance in Minnesota, and the two married in 1932. 
* During the early years of their marriage, they ran a logging camp, with Gladys serving as camp cook—a vital role that kept the men fed and the operation running smoothly.
* In 1937, the Petrie family moved west, passing through Conrad, Montana, and Wallace, Idaho, before finally settling in Spokane, Washington.
* Gladys pursued a career as an Operating Room Nurse.
* She frequently visited Bemidji, Minnesota, to see extended family
* Gladys Cooper lived through the Dust Bowl, World War II, and the rise of modern America. She was remembered as a devoted mother, hardworking nurse, and fiercely independent woman who balanced warmth with backbone.
