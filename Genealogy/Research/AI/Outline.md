2\. Narrative Structure (Recommended Outline)

You can organize the family history using a Five-Part Chronological and Thematic Framework, which works especially well if you’re covering families like Petri, Daubel, Cooper, Strong, Helbig, Bartosh, Rudolph, and Spence:

Can you write Part I with regional context and life up to but not including the emigration to America.

Part I – European Roots

•	Chapters grouped by original homelands: Luxembourg, Germany, England, Czech Republic, etc.

•	Provide historical context: economic, political, and religious factors prompting emigration

•	Introduce each primary family branch:

•	Petri Family in Rumelange, Luxembourg

•	Daubel Family in Bonfeld, Württemberg

•	Cooper and Strong Families in Pennsylvania and New England

•	Bartosh and Reha in Bohemia and Iowa

•	Rudolphs in Bempflingen

•	Spence family in Yorkshire

Can you rewrite Part II with regional context of each initial settlement, before further westward migration, and information about biographies in that context.

Part II – Migration to America - First Settlements

•	Ship manifests, settlement records

•	Explain immigration processes (e.g., Barge Office, Ellis Island, Castle Garden)

•	Describe first settlements:

•	Avon and Liberty Township, MN (Petris)

•	Hanover, KS (Daubels)

•	Iowa (Bartosh and Reha)

•	Philadelphia, PA (Rudolph)

•	Santa Fe, NM (Helbigs)

•	Defiance, OH (Spence)

Can you rewrite Part III with information about biographies in that context.

Part III – Building a Life on the Frontier

•	Homesteading, farming, logging, and early careers

•	Community life, churches, schools, post offices

•	Individual biographies placed in context:

Matthias Petri and Family – Pioneers of Liberty Township, Minnesota

John Petri and Gertrude Helbig – From Santa Fe, NM to Pioneers of Liberty Township, Minnesota

Ludwig Daubel - blacksmith and pioneer settler from KS to WA

Charles and Marie Daubel – Builders of the Elk, Washington Homestead and their descendants

Frank and Mary Bartosh – Czech Farmers on the Dakota Plains

Pennsylvania and New York to Dakota to Minnesota migrations (Coopers, Strongs)

John Spence – Frontier teamster from Yorkshire to Washington

Robert Helbig’s work at the Indian School


Part IV – Westward to Spokane

•	The great migration to Washington state

•	Life in Elk, Milan, Wallace, Spokane

•	Logging camps, farming, and industrial work

•	Focus on families like:

Robert Petrie and Gladys Cooper – From Logging Camps to Spokane’s Urban Life

Herman Daubel and Theresa Strong – Woodsmen, Farmers, and Modern Workers

Part V – Descendants and Legacy

•	The children, grandchildren, and family lines today

•	DNA discoveries and reunions (like Melanie Teems)

•	Maps and family trees

•	Reflections on identity, heritage, and continuity
