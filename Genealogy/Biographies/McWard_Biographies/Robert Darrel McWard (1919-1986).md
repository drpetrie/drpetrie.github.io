# Robert Darrel McWard (1919-1986)

## Early Life and Family Background

Robert Darrel McWard was born in 1919 in Taylorville, the son of Elmer McWard, a long-serving county official and sheriff, and Helen Hobson. He grew up in a household deeply shaped by public service. During his father’s tenure as sheriff, the family lived in close proximity to the courthouse and jail, placing Robert’s childhood squarely within the civic and legal life of Christian County.

## Education and Formative Years

Robert came of age during the interwar period and the Great Depression, years that emphasized discipline, responsibility, and public duty. These early influences, combined with his father’s example in county government and law enforcement, steered him toward a career rooted in law and public service.

## Military Service in World War II

During World War II, Robert served in the United States Navy. His wartime service placed him among the generation whose lives were interrupted by global conflict and then reshaped by it. Military service reinforced values of order, accountability, and leadership that would later define his civilian career.

## Legal Career

Following the war, Robert returned to Illinois and pursued the legal profession. He established himself as a lawyer in Taylorville, entering a profession closely aligned with his family’s longstanding role in county governance. His legal work placed him at the center of community affairs, representing clients and engaging daily with the local court system.

## Service as a Judge

Robert later advanced to the bench, serving as a judge in Taylorville. In this role, he transitioned from advocate to arbiter, entrusted with interpreting and applying the law impartially. His judgeship represented the culmination of a life shaped by law, order, and civic responsibility—continuing the McWard family’s legacy of public service across generations.

## Marriage and Family

Robert married Marjorie White, and together they raised two children, Robert McWard and Ellen McWard. Family life remained anchored in Taylorville, reinforcing strong ties to the community that had shaped multiple generations of the McWard family.

## Migration and Later Years

Although Robert spent time living in Arizona, he ultimately returned to Illinois, reflecting a lasting attachment to his hometown and professional roots. His life followed a clear arc—Illinois to Arizona and back again—mirroring a temporary departure rather than a permanent break from home.

## Death and Legacy

Robert Darrel McWard died in 1986 in Taylorville. Remembered as a veteran, lawyer, and judge, his life exemplified continuity in public service. From his father’s role as sheriff and county official to his own service on the bench, Robert carried forward a tradition of civic leadership that left a durable imprint on Taylorville and Christian County.
